The Waterfall
Concept
The waterfall is the way finplan structures your household finances. All your net income flows in at the top. First, your committed spend is deducted — the bills and contracts you can't easily stop. Then your discretionary spend — the choices you make each month. Whatever remains is your surplus. Example: If your household takes home £4,200/month, commits £2,100 to mortgage, bills and contracts, and budgets £1,400 for food, transport, and subscriptions — your surplus is £700.

Why it matters in finplan
The waterfall isn't just a concept — it's the structure of the entire app. The Overview page shows your waterfall as a live summary. When you add an income source, a bill, or a discretionary budget, you're building this cascade. The surplus at the bottom updates automatically, giving you an always-current picture of what's left to save or invest.
