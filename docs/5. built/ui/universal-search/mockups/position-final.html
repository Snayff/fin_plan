Two decisions: icon position + open behaviour

Resolving both lets us lock the trigger and open animation into the design.

1. Where does the trigger icon sit?

Both options show the same open behaviour (anchored overlay) for consistent comparison.

L

Left — right after the brand

Signals "primary, universal". Matches GitHub, Stripe, Vercel conventions. Separates utility from user/household context on the right.

R

Right — before the household switcher

Groups with other chrome icons (household, avatar). Reads as "utility" rather than "primary nav". Doesn't compete with the main nav row on the left.

2. How does the search appear when opened?

Both options: input+results in one card, rest of page dimmed, Escape closes. The difference is where the card sits on screen.

E

Expand anchored — drops from the top

Card attaches under the top bar and spans most of the viewport width. Feels like the trigger is "blooming" into the search. Closer to your Spotify reference.

C

Centre modal — floats in middle

Classic Raycast/Cmd-K style. Card centred on screen, narrower than full-width. More focused, but detached from the trigger position.
